About Me

At the end of my first year at Oregon State University, I stumbled across a TED talk about flag design. This lead me to a podcast called 99% invisible, a series about all the design elements that go unnoticed in our modern world. The conversation on design was a breath of fresh air from the math and C++ programing courses that dominated the last 6 months of my life. From then on, I became obsessed with focusing my studies in computer science on making our electronic world feel more human.

In my sophomore year, I created a custom focus in Human Computer Interactions and Graphic Design to learn how to translate the power of computation into a form that was approachable, understandable and beautiful. My web development experience has taught me how to build a product that is friendly to both a user and a developer, and I seek to apply those skills towards work that improves people’s lives.

Work

Ninkasi September 2017 - Present
Software Developer Eugene, OR

Ninkasi Brewing Company is based in Eugene, Oregon, producing and distributing nearly 100,000 barrels of beer each year across the United States and Canada. The company has grown rapidly over the last few years and their success has been difficult to keep up with. The process they were using to manage the data generated by brewing was slow and time consuming. Two fellow students and I reached out to the brewmaster at the company to inform them about the senior project that our University hosts. Over the course of our senior year, a team of three computer science students get together and design, implement and release software to fit the needs of the client.

Our team has taken full advantage of this opportunity and we have been continuously providing work that has over delivered on both our client and instructor’s expectations. During our winter break in 2017, I personally corresponded with the brewmaster at Ninkasi and went through several iterations of design for their user interface. I created the mockups in Adobe XD, and after reaching a prototype that both the brewmaster and I were happy with, I visited the company headquarters on my own to both demonstrate what I had built and to get feedback from a focus group of brewers and managers at Ninkasi. I gave the presentation, ran through use cases and found several changes that needed to be made to both the database and the user interface. I implemented the changes during the meeting, and left with a version that everyone was excited about.

Since then, I have implemented the web user interface on our project using webpack, html, stylus, and Vue.js to create a web app responsive to both desktop and mobile devices. We are still in the process of developing the product, and expect to have the product ready to deploy within the company by the end of spring.

Physics Research Project June 2016 - October 2016
Lead Web Developer Corvallis, OR

While working as a Teacher’s Assistant (TA) for the Intro to CS course, and as the front-end developer on a graduate student startup project, I was contacted by a physics professor that was interested in creating an open source physics textbook for his students and releasing it on an online platform. He had received an educational grant to develop his book, and he hired six physics students to build the content of his book. He still needed a developer, so he reached out to other professors around the university in the computer science department. I was the first recommendation for his project and he contacted me asking if I could work with him on his project.

I joined the team when summer break began in June, and worked through September on implementing the content generated by the physics students. They needed the site to run in Drupal 7 so it could make use of the data tracking modules for research on how to improve the textbook. We finished the project before his fall courses began and he has been using it for the last year and a half. He needed occasional fixes through December and I was happy to help though the funding ran out at the end of September. I believed strongly in the idea to create a fun, interactive and free text-book, so I offered my services whenever he needed it and I had the time to help.

Intro to CS TA September 2015 - December 2017
Teachers Assitant Corvallis, OR

My longest running job has been working as a TA for the Intro to Computer Science courses at Oregon State University. There are three courses, CS 160, 161, 162, each one building directly on the other and taught by a small group of instructors that bring a unified experience to the year long series. CS 160 is an introduction to programming with a focus on concepts and beginning to program with python. CS 161 focuses on programming in C++, and though it is often tough for most students to work with a relatively low-level programming language such as C++, they gain a deeper understanding of the mechanics of programing as a field. CS 162 is an introduction to object oriented programing with C++.

All CS majors are required to take these courses, and a large number of students take the 161 and 162 courses. As soon as I finished CS 162, I began working as a TA for 161. At that point, there were around 15 TAs for the course, and we did everything from grading, to recitation and lab supervision. Over the terms the course expanded to requiring recitations, administering coding exams at the end of the term, a staff of over 30 TAs and the cap on students doubling from about 200 to a little over 400 students. I worked as a TA for every fall, winter and spring term from fall 2015 to fall 2017, taking only one term off.

Over the course of my tenure, I became increasingly more involved in curriculum planning and offering resources to both the students and TAs. I wrote a grading script in bash to streamline the grading process, I redesigned the website to be mobile friendly, and wrote some web-scraping python scripts to make the process of scheduling TAs easier. As an officer of the App Development Club, Linux Users Group and Association of Computing Machinery, I invited students to engage both in the course, as well as the social and educational environments of the clubs.

I have a passion for learning and teaching that has driven me throughout my academic career at Oregon State University. I took the job as a TA in the hope that I could be a person that made a significant positive impact on all students, particularly those who, like me, are passionate about their studies and work.
